Space Shuttles



Columbia

Space Shuttle Columbia was the first of NASAs Space Shuttle fleet. It
operated for 22 years and completed 27 missions. Unfortunately, during
re-entry at the end of its 28 th mission, a missing heat shield caused the
left wing to burn up, causing an explosion. All seven people on board were
killed.

Challenger

Challenger was NASAs second orbiter. Sadly, it broke apart 73 seconds
after launch on its tenth flight, causing a two-and-a-half year grounding
of the shuttle program; until Discovery first flew on its first mission in
1988.

Discovery

Discovery was NASAs third orbiter. It was the first space shuttle to
retire without getting destroyed. Over a span of 27 years, it flew
a total of 39 missions.Discovery gave NASA hope that the following
space shuttles would also survive.

Atlantis

Atlantis was the fourth shuttle in the space shuttle fleet. It flew
for 26 years and completed a total of 33 missions. Its last
flight, STS-135, was also the the spaca shuttle programs last
mission.

Endeavour

Endeavour was the last space shuttle of NASAs space shuttle fleet. It
flew for a total of 19 years, and completed 25 missions. Its main
purpose was to replace Challenger,which was destroyed in 1986.
